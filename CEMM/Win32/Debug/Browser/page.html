Descripción
"War is the struggle to exist. Everything real is a combatant."

- Future War Cult, Destiny


Welcome!

Hello everyone,

With this mod, I aim to improve upon Conan Exiles' combat system.

By performing data table mergers with DT_ComboRules and WeaponUseTable via a ModController, I am able to accomplish the following:

* Animation adjustments and replacements.

* Combo restructuring.

* Damage and effect balancing.

The end result is the creation of a new combat paradigm.


What the Mod Changes

Going into each minute change here would be impossible as there's simply not enough space. I'll try to keep it brief and hit the highlights:

* General - hundreds of various adjustments to attack animations and their notifiers spread across all weapon classes. Full damage, stamina cost, and status effect rebalancing at the weapon class level. Replaced the Offhand kicks for all 1H weapons. Added 4-part Shield Bash Heavy combo for all 1H weapons. Colored weapon trails have been removed and replaced with the 'air-cutting' effect.

* 2H Axe - full animation rework using the 2H Mace combos. The old Heavy spin 'Whirlwind' attacks have been sped up and moved to become a Heavy finisher sequence performed after Light 3.

* 2H Mace - addition of the Whirlwind attacks to the alternate Heavy finisher position.

* 2H Spear - re-implemented the old Light 'Sweeper' combo, replacing the stationary combo. Optional Offhand sweep finisher added to both combos.

* 2H Sword - combo sequences restructed with re-implementation of the old Heavy opener and finisher. The Offhand kick has returned. Optional Offhand sweep finisher added to both combos.

* Bow - Heavy shot draw speed has been increased by 50%. A backwards hop has been added into the Offhand slot.

* Daggers - Offhand backflip has been replaced with the backwards hop. The hops do not reset the combo if timed correctly, allowing for quick hit-and-run tactics. Incorporated the flurry combo as a Light sequence after Heavy 3.

* 1H Axe - replacement of the old Light combo with the 'Standardized' combo, essentially a modified 1H Sword Light combo.

* 1H Mace - replaced the Light combo with the Standardized combo.

* 1H Spear - replaced the Light combo with various attack animations. Heavy Shield Bash and Throwing Axe combo support has been added.

* 1H Sword - replaced the Light combo with the Standardized combo. The Heavy finisher has been replaced with its pre- Blood and Sand counterpart.

* Shortsword - added Shield Bash and Throwing Axe Heavy support. Implemented the Standardized Light combo when a Throwing Axe is equipped.

* Truncheon - all attacks have been replaced with the standardized combo. Thank me later. Shield Bash support has been incorporated.

Refer to the other documentation provided (Change Logs and specific Discussion tabs) for more complete details.

Damage Balancing:

I primarily use TTK calculations in determining buffs and nerfs with regards to damage multipliers, as that statistic strongly correlates with real DPS output. I have separated weapons into groups (1H, 2H, Ranged, etc.) and tightened the aggregate TTK ranges of those weapons against other members of their group. Additionally, I have stratified each group's TTKs to create a clear hierarchy of damage output, with 2H weapons at the top, followed by 1H Special attacks (Shield Bash, Throwing Axe, Mounted), 1H Normal, and Ranged.

(NOTE: My TTK calculations involve using 4 tiers of a weapon [iron, steel, hardened steel, star metal/epic] against on-level T1 and T3 Fighter NPCs [Black Hand, Dogs, Heirs, Forgotten Tribe] with no Strength or Accuracy investments.)

I have not touched individual weapon stats (base damage) in this mod. There will be outliers in every tier due to Funcom's choices in designing weapons.

Glory was initially designed for my personal use as a solo player. There will likely be eccentricities which may negatively impact PvP scenarios. You have been warned.

Mod Compatibility

I have accomplished the above by merging data tables with DT_ComboRules and WeaponUseTable. In addition, all animations used by Glory are modified copies of vanilla animations, which plug in to the combat system via the table mergers. This makes Glory compatible with a wide range of mods, even those which also modify the combat system.

If, for one reason or another, you do not like what I did with a particular weapon, it is possible to override that portion of my mod with another. Hint hint.

You must ensure that the other mod is lower in the load order than mine for their changes to appear. Double check when you move mods around in the load order.

Known incompatible mods will be listed in Discussions as they are reported.


Conclusion

Thank you all for reading,

- Dread Swoop

Dread Swoop  [autor] 25 MAR a las 11:27 
@Morphine I did some digging around to report on a Funcom Forum thread about it and here's what I found:

Funcom deleted the entries for Katana Heavy 2, 3, + 4 from the combo table in 2.3. The IDs just weren't there. The ComboStepNext entries for the Light attacks had the references to the Heavy attacks removed. This obviously caused alternations from Light to Heavy to reset the combo, in addition to preventing the Heavy finisher from performing.

Funcom addressed this in the hotfix by re-adding the IDs, but the finisher animation was absent, implying that Funcom copy / pasted Heavy 1 and then just re-wired everything to allow the combo to be uninterrupted.

They haven't said whether this is intentional or not, but from my experience working in that sphere of the game, IDs don't just vanish. CSN entries don't magically rearrange themselves. It's almost certainly deliberate.
